# Market Context — MathBattle

**Version:** 1.0.0

---

## Market Opportunity

The global EdTech market is projected to exceed $400 billion by 2028. Mathematics learning tools represent one of the largest and most consistently funded segments, driven by persistent underperformance in standardized assessments across K–12 systems globally.

Key market drivers:
- Post-pandemic acceleration of digital learning adoption in schools
- Increasing teacher workload and shortage of qualified mathematics educators
- Growing institutional demand for measurable learning outcomes and compliance reporting
- AI tooling reducing the cost and time of curriculum content creation

---

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses vs. MathBattle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Khan Academy | Free learning platform | Broad content library, trusted brand | No multiplayer, no teacher question bank, no AI generation |
| Prodigy Math | Gamified math game | High student engagement | Game-first (not curriculum-first), limited teacher tools |
| IXL Learning | Adaptive practice | Strong curriculum alignment | No gamification, no multiplayer, expensive |
| Quizlet | Flashcard/study tool | Easy content creation | Not mathematics-specific, no structured learning paths |
| Kahoot | Game-based assessment | High engagement in classroom | No individual learning paths, no AI, no match system |
| Mathletics | School math platform | Established in schools | Dated UX, limited AI, no hidden-card match system |
| DeltaMath | Teacher-focused math tool | Strong teacher tools | No student gamification, no AI generation |

---

## MathBattle Differentiation

MathBattle is the only platform that combines:
1. Curriculum-aligned structured learning paths (not a game)
2. AI-powered question generation and weakness detection
3. Competitive multiplayer matches with educational scoring mechanics
4. Separate, purpose-built dashboards for students, teachers, and administrators
5. Full question metadata (Bloom's taxonomy, difficulty, version history)

---

## Target Market Segments

### Primary: K–12 Schools (Institutional)
- Decision maker: Principal or Head of Mathematics Department
- Budget holder: School or district administration
- Sales motion: Pilot program → school license → district expansion
- Key buying criteria: Curriculum alignment, teacher time savings, measurable outcomes, data privacy

### Secondary: Private Tutoring Centers
- Decision maker: Center owner or director
- Budget holder: Center owner
- Sales motion: Direct sign-up, monthly subscription
- Key buying criteria: Student engagement, progress tracking, ease of use

### Tertiary: Homeschool Networks
- Decision maker: Parent
- Budget holder: Parent
- Sales motion: Word of mouth, community referral
- Key buying criteria: Curriculum alignment, student engagement, affordability

## Regulatory Environment

| Regulation | Applicability | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| COPPA (US) | Students under 13 | Parental consent, data minimization, no behavioral advertising |
| FERPA (US) | K–12 student records | School controls student data; no disclosure without consent |
| GDPR (EU) | EU students and teachers | Data minimization, right to erasure, DPA with processors |
| PIPEDA (Canada) | Canadian users | Consent, access, correction rights |
| UK GDPR | UK users | Similar to EU GDPR post-Brexit |
